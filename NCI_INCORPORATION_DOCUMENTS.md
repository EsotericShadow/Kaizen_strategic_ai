## 1. Articles of Incorporation

### Organization Name
**Northern Cascadia Institute of AI Governance**  
(Also operating as: **NCI**)

### Purpose and Objectives
The Northern Cascadia Institute of AI Governance is a nonprofit organization established to:

1. **Develop and publish regional AI governance standards** aligned with international frameworks (ISO 42001:2023) for Northern BC and beyond
2. **Promote practical, accessible AI governance tools** designed for smaller organizations, remote communities, and regional businesses
3. **Host the Annual AI & Technology Summit** bringing together business leaders, government officials, Indigenous representatives, and technology experts
4. **Engage in community consultation** with Indigenous governments, municipalities, corporations, and stakeholders
5. **Advance public interest** in AI governance through education, standards development, and public engagement
6. **Support adoption** of AI governance standards through resources, templates, and guidance

### Structure Type
**Model B+C** - Nonprofit Society with Charitable Purposes Component

### Legal Status
- **Type:** BC Society (Nonprofit)
- **Corporate Classification:** Public Interest Organization
- **Tax Status:** Pending charitable registration (CRA)

## 6. Standards Development Process

### Public Comment Framework
1. **Draft Publication:** Standards published as "Draft for Public Comment"
2. **Comment Period:** Open invitation to:
   - Indigenous governments
   - Municipal governments
   - Corporations and stakeholders
   - Professional associations
   - Academic institutions
3. **Review Process:** Board reviews all comments
4. **Revision:** Standards updated based on feedback
5. **Final Publication:** Published as official NCI standards

### Current Standards
- **NC-AI-001:** AI Risk Register Template (v1.0 - Draft for Public Comment)
