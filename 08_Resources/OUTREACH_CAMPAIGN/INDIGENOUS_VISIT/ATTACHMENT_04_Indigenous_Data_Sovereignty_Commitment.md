# NCI Commitment to Indigenous Data Sovereignty

**For**: First Nations Governments and Organizations  
**Purpose**: Clear statement of our commitment and approach  
**Status**: Core Principle

---

## Our Commitment

**Northern Cascadia Institute of AI Governance (NCI) is committed to respecting and supporting Indigenous data sovereignty in all our work.**

This is not optional - it is foundational to who we are and what we do.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Respect for Indigenous Sovereignty

**We Recognize**:
- Indigenous governments have jurisdiction over AI governance on their territories
- Indigenous nations have sovereignty over their data
- Traditional knowledge belongs to Indigenous communities
- Indigenous protocols must be respected

**We Commit**:
- Never assume we know what Indigenous organizations need
- Always ask permission before using Indigenous data or knowledge
- Respect Indigenous decision-making processes
- Follow Indigenous leadership, not provide direction

---

### 2. OCAP® Principles Alignment

**OCAP® Principles**:
- **Ownership**: Indigenous nations own their data
- **Control**: Indigenous nations control data collection and use
- **Access**: Indigenous nations access their data
- **Possession**: Indigenous nations physically possess their data

**Our Alignment**:
- All NCI standards respect OCAP® principles
- NC-AI-001 includes Indigenous considerations
- NC-AI-006 (planned) will be built on OCAP® principles
- We advocate for Indigenous data sovereignty in all AI governance

---

### 3. Partnership Approach

**We Don't**:
- Dictate what Indigenous organizations should do
- Assume we know what's needed
- Create standards without Indigenous input
- Claim authority over Indigenous matters

**We Do**:
- Partner with Indigenous organizations
- Collaborate on standards development
- Learn from Indigenous expertise
- Co-develop frameworks together

---

### 4. Traditional Knowledge Protection

**We Respect**:
- Traditional knowledge belongs to Indigenous communities
- Cultural protocols must be followed
- Traditional knowledge cannot be exploited
- Indigenous communities control their knowledge

**We Protect**:
- Never use traditional knowledge without consent
- Never publish Indigenous knowledge without permission
- Respect cultural protocols
- Support Indigenous control over knowledge

---

### 5. Data Sovereignty in AI

**AI Systems Must**:
- Respect Indigenous data ownership
- Support Indigenous control over data
- Enable Indigenous access to their data
- Allow Indigenous possession of data

**Our Standards**:
- Require consent for AI training data
- Support local/sovereign data storage
- Enable Indigenous control over AI systems
- Respect Indigenous decision-making

---

## Implementation in Our Standards

### NC-AI-001 (Current)
- Includes Indigenous considerations in risk categories
- References data sovereignty
- Acknowledges Indigenous jurisdiction
- Free for Indigenous organizations

### NC-AI-006 (Planned)
- Co-developed with Indigenous partners
- Built on OCAP® principles
- Indigenous-led development
- Comprehensive data sovereignty framework

### All Future Standards
- Will include Indigenous considerations
- Will require Indigenous input
- Will respect sovereignty
