# Partnership Proposal: NCI and Indigenous Organizations

**For**: First Nations Governments and Organizations  
**Purpose**: Co-development of Indigenous-inclusive AI governance standards  
**Status**: Draft Proposal

---

## Partnership Overview

**What We're Proposing**: Collaborative partnership to develop AI governance standards that respect and support Indigenous data sovereignty.

**Approach**: Partnership, collaboration, co-development - not direction or prescription.

**Timeline**: Ongoing, long-term relationship

---

## Partnership Opportunities

### 1. Co-Development of NC-AI-006: Indigenous Data Sovereignty Protocol

**What**: Develop a comprehensive standard for AI governance that integrates:
- OCAP® principles (Ownership, Control, Access, Possession)
- Indigenous data sovereignty
- Traditional knowledge protection
- Community-controlled AI systems

**How**: 
- Indigenous partners lead development
- NCI provides framework and technical support
- Co-authorship on final standard
- Indigenous approval before publication

**Timeline**: 6-12 months (depending on partnership structure)

**Benefits**:
- Indigenous-led standard
- Free for all Indigenous organizations
- Practical tool for AI governance
- Regional and national applicability

---

### 2. Input and Review on NC-AI-001

**What**: Review current NC-AI-001 template for Indigenous contexts

**How**:
- Review template from Indigenous data sovereignty perspective
- Provide feedback on gaps or concerns
- Suggest improvements for Indigenous organizations
- Influence future versions

**Timeline**: Ongoing

**Benefits**:
- Standards that work for Indigenous organizations
- Free tool adapted for Indigenous contexts
- Your perspective shapes future standards

---

### 3. Joint Workshops and Training

**What**: Collaborative workshops on AI governance for Indigenous communities

**How**:
- Co-host workshops with Indigenous partners
- Indigenous-led content where appropriate
- Practical training using NC-AI-001
- Community-specific adaptations

**Timeline**: On-demand, as needed

**Benefits**:
- Community education on AI governance
- Practical tools for implementation
- Relationship building
- Knowledge sharing

---

### 4. Case Studies and Documentation

**What**: Document Indigenous AI governance implementations

**How**:
- Case studies of Indigenous organizations using standards
- Success stories (with permission)
- Best practices documentation
- Regional examples

**Timeline**: Ongoing

**Benefits**:
- Showcase Indigenous leadership
- Share best practices
- Build credibility
- Support other Indigenous organizations

## Partnership Structure

### Collaborative Development
- **Indigenous Leadership**: Indigenous partners lead development
- **NCI Support**: NCI provides framework, technical support, resources
- **Co-Authorship**: Indigenous partners as co-authors
- **Approval Process**: Indigenous approval required before publication

### Communication
- **Regular Updates**: Progress updates on standards development
- **Feedback Loops**: Continuous input and feedback
- **Transparency**: Open about process and decisions
- **Respect**: Acknowledge Indigenous expertise and sovereignty

### Benefits Sharing
- **Free Tools**: All standards remain free (public domain)
- **Credit**: Indigenous partners credited as co-developers
- **Distribution**: Joint distribution through both networks
- **Recognition**: Public acknowledgment of partnership
