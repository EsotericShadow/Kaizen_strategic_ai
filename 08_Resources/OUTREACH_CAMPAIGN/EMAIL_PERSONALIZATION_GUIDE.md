# Email Personalization Guide

**Purpose**: Customize each email template before sending to increase response rates

---

## Pre-Send Customization Checklist

### 1. Verify Contact Information
- [ ] Confirm email address is current (check website)
- [ ] Verify contact name and title
- [ ] Check for recent organization news/changes

### 2. Research Recipient Organization
- [ ] Recent achievements or news
- [ ] Current priorities or initiatives
- [ ] Specific projects related to AI/governance
- [ ] Key people or departments

### 3. Personalize Email Content

#### Subject Line
- [ ] Include organization name
- [ ] Reference specific initiative (if known)
- [ ] Keep concise and compelling
- **Examples**:
  - "Partnership Opportunity: AI Governance Standards with FNTC"
  - "Local Collaboration: AI Governance Tools for City of Terrace"
  - "Educational Partnership: AI Training at Coast Mountain College"

#### Opening Paragraph
- [ ] Reference specific achievement or initiative
- [ ] Mention recent news or project
- [ ] Show genuine interest and research
- **Examples**:
  - "I noticed FNTC's AI needs survey for First Nations..."
  - "Congratulations on Bulkley-Nechako's AI Governance Policy..."
  - "I saw Coast Mountain College's commitment to community education..."

#### Body Content
- [ ] Adjust examples to match organization type
- [ ] Reference specific alignment points
- [ ] Customize partnership opportunities
- [ ] Match tone to organization culture

#### Closing
- [ ] Personalize call to action
- [ ] Reference local connection (if applicable)
- [ ] Show flexibility and respect

## General Personalization Rules

### Do's
✅ Reference specific initiatives or achievements  
✅ Show you've researched the organization  
✅ Match tone to organization culture  
✅ Use recipient's name (if known)  
✅ Reference mutual connections (if any)  
✅ Be specific about why you're reaching out

### Don'ts
❌ Generic openings ("I hope this email finds you well")  
❌ Copy-paste without customization  
❌ Assumptions about organization priorities  
❌ Overly casual or overly formal  
❌ Long emails (keep under 300 words)  
❌ Multiple calls to action (focus on one)

---

## Timing Considerations

- **Weekday Mornings**: Best for professional organizations
- **Tuesday-Thursday**: Highest response rates
- **Avoid**: Mondays (busy), Fridays (low attention)
- **Time**: 9-11 AM or 2-4 PM local time

---

## Follow-Up Personalization

If no response after 1 week:
- Reference original email date
- Add new information (e.g., "We've updated NC-AI-001 based on feedback...")
- Lower pressure ("If this isn't timely right now...")
- Offer alternatives ("Would you prefer I reach out in [future time]?")
