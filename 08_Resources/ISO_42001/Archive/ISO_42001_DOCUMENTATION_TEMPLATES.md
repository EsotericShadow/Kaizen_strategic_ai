## 1. Risk Identification

**When**: 
- New AI systems introduced
- Changes to existing AI systems
- Quarterly reviews
- After incidents

**Sources**:
- AI system characteristics
- Data used
- Stakeholder impact
- Legal/regulatory requirements
- Technology risks
- Third-party dependencies

**Methods**:
- Brainstorming sessions
- Review of system documentation
- Client feedback
- Incident analysis

---

## 2. Risk Assessment

**Impact Categories**:
- **Critical**: Severe impact on operations, clients, or compliance
- **High**: Significant impact requiring immediate attention
- **Medium**: Moderate impact with standard controls
- **Low**: Minor impact with routine monitoring

**Likelihood Categories**:
- **Almost Certain**: >90% probability
- **Likely**: 50-90% probability
- **Possible**: 10-50% probability
- **Rare**: <10% probability

**Risk Matrix**:
[Use risk assessment matrix from gap analysis]

---

## 3. Risk Evaluation

**Risk Levels**:
- **Critical Risk**: Immediate action required
- **High Risk**: Priority mitigation within 30 days
- **Medium Risk**: Standard controls within 90 days
- **Low Risk**: Accept or monitor

**Acceptance Criteria**:
- All critical and high risks must be mitigated
- Medium risks require documented controls
- Low risks can be accepted with monitoring

---

## 4. Risk Treatment

**Treatment Options**:
- **Avoid**: Eliminate the risk
- **Mitigate**: Reduce likelihood or impact
- **Transfer**: Shift risk to third party
- **Accept**: Acknowledge and monitor

**Mitigation Measures**:
- Technical controls
- Process improvements
- Training and awareness
- Monitoring and testing
- Insurance coverage

---

## 5. Risk Monitoring

**Frequency**: 
- Critical risks: Monthly
- High risks: Quarterly
- Medium/Low risks: Annually

**Review**: 
- Annual comprehensive review
- After significant changes
- After incidents
- Before certification audits

---

## 6. Documentation

**Records Maintained**:
- Risk Register (Excel/Google Sheets)
- Risk Assessment Reports
- Mitigation Plans
- Monitoring Results
- Review Minutes

**Retention**: 5 years or as required by regulation

# AI Impact Assessment Procedure

**Purpose**: Evaluate potential impacts of AI systems on stakeholders and society.

**Scope**: All new and modified AI systems at Kaizen Strategic AI.

**Responsibility**: Gabriel Lacroix, Project Owner

---

## 1. When to Conduct Assessment

**Triggered By**:
- New AI system development
- Significant AI system modifications
- New data types or sources
- Changes in AI system purpose
- Client concerns or complaints

**Frequency**: As needed, before deployment

---

## 2. Impact Categories

**Stakeholder Impacts**:
- Clients and end users
- Employees and contractors
- Business partners
- Regulatory bodies
- General public

**Impact Types**:
- Safety and security
- Privacy and data protection
- Fairness and discrimination
- Transparency and explainability
- Human rights and dignity

---

## 3. Assessment Process

**Step 1: Preparation**
- Assemble assessment team
- Gather system documentation
- Identify stakeholders
- Review similar systems

**Step 2: Impact Analysis**
- Identify potential impacts
- Assess likelihood
- Evaluate severity
- Consider cumulative effects

**Step 3: Stakeholder Consultation**
- Engage affected parties
- Gather feedback
- Address concerns
- Document input

**Step 4: Documentation**
- Complete assessment template
- Record findings
- Document decisions
- Create mitigation plan

---

## 4. Impact Assessment Template

**System Information**:
- System name and purpose
- Technology used
- Data processed
- Stakeholders affected

**Impact Analysis**:
- Positive impacts identified
- Negative impacts identified
- Risk level assigned
- Mitigation measures proposed

**Decision**:
- Approved for deployment
- Approved with conditions
- Requires modification
- Not approved

---

## 5. Mitigation Plan

**Required For**:
- Medium or higher risk impacts
- Negative impacts on stakeholders
- Regulatory concerns

**Contents**:
- Mitigation measures
- Responsible party
- Implementation timeline
- Success criteria
- Monitoring plan

## 6. Review and Approval
