### Decision #26: Realistic Community-Driven Approach
**Context**: Realizing the limits of solo authority and the reality of standards development

**The Realization**:
- "I can't just dictate the standard. Who the fuck am I?"
- "It feels too serious for me, I'm just a pothead"
- "The chances my standard templates are adopted?"
- Need to work WITH ISO, not replace it
- No permission needed - these are templates, not official standards
- Build it, post it professionally, invite comment, version it, let it grow

**Decision**: **Create templates as "Draft for Public Comment" and let adoption determine authority**

**The Realistic Process**:
1. Build professional templates (ISO-compliant)
2. Post them as "Draft for Public Comment"
3. Invite community feedback
4. Version based on comments
5. Let organic adoption establish authority
6. Don't claim authority - earn it through adoption

**Why This Works**:
- Honest about position ("just a pothead" = humble)
- Professional presentation creates credibility
- Public comment invites community ownership
- Versioning shows responsiveness
- Adoption over time = real authority
- No dictating = true standards process

**The Reality Check**:
- Can't just say "I'm the standard"
- Must earn authority through quality and adoption
- Community input essential for legitimacy
- "Just seems like a bunch of pie in the sky bs" = keep it real
- Growth happens organically, not through declaration

**The Approach**:
- Professional and authoritative presentation
- Open to feedback and iteration
- Version control for changes
- Let community decide if it's useful
- Build it, invite people, establish through adoption

**Key Insight**: "I just have to build it and invite people and that will establish me right?" - Yes, through quality + adoption, not declaration.
