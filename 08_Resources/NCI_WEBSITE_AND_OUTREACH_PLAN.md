## Phase 5: Corporate Structure Decision

### Option A: NCI as Subsidiary of Kaizen
**Structure**: NCI is a subsidiary/division of Kaizen Strategic AI  
**Pros**:
- Simpler legal structure
- Kaizen controls operations
- Easier financial management
- Shared resources

**Cons**:
- May appear less independent
- Potential conflict of interest perception
- Non-profit status more complex within for-profit structure

### Option B: Separate Entity
**Structure**: NCI is a completely separate non-profit organization  
**Pros**:
- Clear separation of for-profit and non-profit
- More independent appearance
- Easier to receive grants/funding
- Clear governance structure

**Cons**:
- More complex legal setup (separate incorporation)
- Dual branding/marketing needs
- Separate financial systems

### Recommendation
**Option B (Separate Entity)** is recommended for:
- Clear separation of profit/non-profit missions
- Better grant eligibility
- Perceived independence and authority
- Easier partnerships with government/Indigenous entities

### Legal Requirements (BC, Canada)
- **Non-profit incorporation**: BC Society Act or Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act
- **Separate bank account**
- **Separate board of directors** (if required)
- **Tax-exempt status application** (Charitable organization registration)
